Why We Did It (Theory):
-----------------------
The Conceptual Data Model (Modèle Conceptuel de Données - MCD) represents the high-level business layout of data. It answers the question: "What data does the business care about, and how are the concepts linked?"
- Entities: Represent objects in the business (e.g. Service, Client, Administrator).
- Attributes: Information describing the entities (e.g. Service Description, Client Phone).
- Cardinalities: Rules defining how instances interact (e.g. A Service wraps 0 or many realizations; a single realization belongs to 1 service).
Designing the MCD is critical because it forces us to align on the database structure before writing any SQL code.
